Koshish-"An initiative by youth to make India a better place to live."

The utmost brutality and inhumanity of the numerous rape cases in India has shaken us all to our very core. We blame the police, the government, and the shameless by-passers who ignored such heinous acts and didn't even lift a finger to help those innocent souls. Some of us even blame the victims, criticizing their dressing or their timing or even their attitude! We claim that we know better, we claim to be on the victim’s side, we claim to fight for justice.

But the question is:

What have we done for them?

Offered our supreme criticism? Pointed out the things that went wrong?

Organized or attended a candle march?

Do you think this is all we could do?

We do street plays on corruption, Use abusive language for politicians.

But we must ask ourselves, what have we done for our nation?

We criticize our municipality for unkempt and filthy lanes, dirty water and foul smell. What have we done to keep our roads clean?

Why have we, the youth, forgotten that We are the wealth of nation. We are the real heroes. We can be the change we wish to see in this world. We have the power to make the impossible possible.

The power to bring the change or atleast try to bring the same is in our hands!

And so here is a social initiative, a social endeavor called "KOSHISH" by ISTE-ITNU as a part of I-FEST’14 in which we wish to make a difference to the society by making the individuals realize that up to what extent can our KOSHISH bring a change in the nation.
