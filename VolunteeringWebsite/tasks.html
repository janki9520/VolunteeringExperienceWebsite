A Small Contribution Towards The Society By Team Sangharsh
In order to provide a helping hand to these poor ones, we the “SANGHARSH” group took an initiative to donate unwanted clothes & other things like shoes, stationary, games etc. to underprivileged people. We held a Cloth Donation Camp for gathering clothes & other accessories from nearby societies. The collected clothes were distributed amongst the slum people, construction site families & also to the charity haat of BPA (Blind People’s Association).
Some of the members from our group visited the ‘JEEVAN TIRTH’ slum area to find the hygiene problems faced by women. There we found that the women worked as replica i.e. they separated plastic metals etc. from garbage. They worked from morning 6 am till noon 2 -3 pm. They separated tons of garbage just to earn 60-70 rupees a day. They did this work with bare hands and without covering their nose.

We came to a conclusion that these women did not have proper knowledge and needed counselling regarding the health issues which these ladies can face if they continued this way. So we decided to conduct our hygiene problem in slum area task in JEEVAN TIRTH for these replica’s.
Hand sanitizers have become popular over the past several years. In addition to popularity, they are also an important component of a healthy lifestyle. Infections are spread by germs and bacteria, and a combination of the use of water, soap and hand sanitizer can go a long way in preventing the spread of germs. Hand sanitizer is portable and convenient when soap and water are not available, and it is often placed in stationary dispensers in places such as schools and hospitals. Hand sanitizers are available in liquid, gel or foam formulations.

We the members SANGHARSH group visited Thaltej Prathmic School for a survey. There we found that students were playing in mud and sand. With the same hands they ate their meals. So we decided to perform the Hand Sanitization task there as we realized that it was really needed there
Initially, we took this initiative from a competition point of view. Later on, we started getting attached to each and every task that we did for the underprivileged people. The true happiness is sharing your happiness with such underprivileged people who crave for our support and little contribution. Seeing them smile gave us alot of satisfaction. This entire one month of social service gave us alot of happiness which will be remembered forever.
With the purpose of spreading happiness, the Sangharsh Group held a visit of orphanage children to old age home on 10th October 2014 (Friday). The visit was held between 9 am and 12 pm. (Approx. 3 hours).

50 children from Gandhi Ashram (Old Wadaj) accompanied by their teachers and Sangharsh group members, were taken to Jeevan Sandhya Old Age Home (Naranpura).

Arrangement of bus was done for the children of Gandhi Ashram Our members decorated the bus with balloons and ribbons so that the children can have a joyful ride.
The street rally on women empowerment was conducted by SANGHARSH group on October 10, 2014 in JEEVAN TIRTH (UNNATI), ram pir no tekro, Juna Vadaj, Ahmedabad.

A day before rally, the members of SANGHARSH group prepared charts based on human empowerment. We made 8 different charts pointing out different issues like dowry, child marriage, save girl child, importance of girl’s education etc.

We prepared a drama on women empowerment to highlight different issues. A drama of 10-12 min was prepared in which almost all the members of SANGHARSH group participated each and every member characterizing a specific role.
The Sangharsh Group took an initiative to conduct a Self Defence Workshop for the underprivileged girls & women of Juna vadaj slum area (Rama pir no tekro). This workshop was organised at Jeevan Tirth NGO (Juna vadaj). The workshop was conducted on 10th Oct 2014 between 12:00 p.m. to 1.00 p.m.

Mr.Dipesh Gajjar was requested to conduct this workshop. He is a very well-known “Karate Master”. He has long years of experience in this field. I personally know him as he was my karate teacher in school. On just one request of mine, he got ready to come to jeevan tirth to share his knowledge regarding Self-defence techniques to the underprivileged girls & women of Juna vadaj slum.

With the purpose of developing sporting spirit in the underprivileged children and bringing some change in their boring routine of studies, the “Sangharsh” group held a sports event on 1st October 2014(Thursday) at “Thaltej Prathmik Shala no.1”. The sports event was conducted between 12:30 pm to 2:30 pm.

We had two of our team members namely; Janki Patel & Shaunak Patadia anchoring throughout the sports event. Our anchors announced all the games that we were going to conduct & patiently explained the rules of the games scheduled and graced the event with their enthusiasm.
Our team members tried to understand some underprivileged families the importance of studies. Also, our team members promised some of the underprivileged kids to pay their municipal school fees for a year. Besides this our team members educated underprivileged families about child labor and their severe outcomes. As child labor is a very common problem often seen in India, we made an initiative to minimize this to a certain extent. Though, we aren't successful to a great level, but we gave our best to educate some of these underprivileged people of India.
After surveying the entire slum area of Juna vadaj, we found out that there were two poor families in the slum who didn’t have toilets at their places since last 10 years. So the SANGHARSH Group took an initiative to install composite toilets for these families in the Juna vadaj slum.

“Khodiyar Traders” provided us a great support in all possible ways. They helped us in developing these toilets & that to free of cost. They also told us that Government provides free of cost toilets to families who don’t have toilets at their places. So with the support of Khodiyar traders we could install these toilets.

Every student should be given the knowledge of planting trees. For the purpose of making understand the municipal school children, the importance of tree plantation, we the Sangharsh Group took an initiative to plant trees on the day of 2nd Oct commonly known as; “SWATCHTA DIVAS”.

The youth of municipal schools and underprivileged children usually have the potential to do something, but they are often deprived of proper counselling and guidance. They do have their goals set in their minds, but do not have the knowledge regarding how to reach their destination. These children usually cannot afford to go to private institutes or learn expensive courses. And due to lack of proper knowledge and guidance they drop out after their secondary and higher secondary schools.

In order to overcome this problem, we the members of ‘SANGHARSH’ group took an initiative to organize a career orientation program for the students of municipal schools studying in X, XI, XII standard. This programme was conducted by Mr. Maulin Pandya and our fellow members.
In order to take one step towards keeping our city clean and healthy, Sangharsh Group held a cleanliness drive on 10th October 2014 near RAMAPIR NO TEKRO BRTS BUSTAND and outside JEEVAN THIRTH (NGO situated near slum areas).Firstly our members had made a survey to find out the places where there was a need of cleanliness drive.

Our team members found that people who live near Ramapir No Tekro BRTS Bus Stand and people who live in the slums near JEEVAN THIRTH throw their house garbage on roads. We started the cleaning session at 4 pm and continued till 5pm.An arrangement of 15 brooms was done by our members. We helped the sweepers to sweep the garbage near BRTS bus stand.
